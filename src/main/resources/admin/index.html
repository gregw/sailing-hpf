Historical Performance Factors

An online database of elapsed times from Australian yacht racing.

The HPF (Historical Performance Factor) is the back-calculated handicap a boat would have needed, averaged across its racing history, to have been equal-time with a hypothetical 1.000 reference boat (roughly calibrated with IRC). It is a measure of historical performance — not a handicap system.

Primary use: Informing the allocation of initial handicaps at season start, or when a new boat joins a series. It facilitates this by showing comparisons between two or more boats on "normalized" corrected times. HPF explicitly does not replace how clubs age or adjust handicaps between races day-to-day.

Public data (boat sail numbers, boat names, club names, and elapsed times) is collected from SailSys and TopYacht results pages and combined with data obtained from publicly available ORC and AMS certificates. Boats with IRC, ORC, or AMS certificates serve as "standard candles" that anchoring the performance factor calculations so they are roughly comparable to current IRC handicaps.
