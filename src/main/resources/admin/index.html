Historical Performance Factors

An online database of elapsed times from Australian yacht racing.

The HPF (Historical Performance Factor) is the back-calculated handicap a boat would have needed, averaged across its racing history, to have been equal-time with a hypothetical 1.000 reference boat. It is a measure of historical performance — not a handicap system.

Primary use: Informing the allocation of initial handicaps at season start, or when a new boat joins a series. HPF explicitly does not replace how clubs age or adjust handicaps between races day-to-day.

Data is collected from SailSys, TopYacht club pages, ORC certificate feeds, and AMS. Boats with IRC, ORC or AMS certificates serve as reference candles, anchoring the comparison between measurement-based and performance-based handicaps.

Import Run data importers and schedule automatic updates from SailSys, TopYacht, ORC and AMS. Data Browser Browse and inspect boats, designs, and race records in the database. Handicap Analysis Compare handicap systems across years and view scatter plots with regression fits and conversion tables.
